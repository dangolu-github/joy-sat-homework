Class summary & reflection

The main work from class and the reading strategy we will continue to practise.

July 17
Covered today We reviewed about half of the most important mistakes from the Class 12 Command of Evidence homework. We then worked through the Class 13 handout from the evidence-to-inference bridge to Evidence Chain and guided Question 1.
What felt difficult Command of Evidence and Inferences both felt difficult, especially when a passage contained long, dense information.
Strategy practised Read the passage as conditional information: if or when X, then Y. Keep the subject, condition, comparison, time, and degree. Connect only the arrows stated in the passage, then choose the smallest conclusion those arrows support.
Reflection The main challenge is becoming used to reading high-density information this way. We have started turning dense passages into short condition chains, and we will keep practising until the process feels more automatic.
Next step For each new passage, say the condition chain aloud and explain what extra evidence the closest distractor would need.
