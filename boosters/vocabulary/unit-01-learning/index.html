Active set · 18 words

adjective

beneficial

producing a useful or positive result

Regular low-intensity exercise can be beneficial to recovery.

adjective

constructive

helpful because it improves or builds something

The reviewer offered constructive criticism, identifying a problem and a way to fix it.

adjective

conducive

making a result easier or more likely

A quiet room is conducive to sustained concentration.

adjective

pragmatic

focused on what is workable in real conditions

With limited funding, the team chose a pragmatic repair instead of a complete redesign.

verb

bolster

support or make stronger

A successful pilot study could bolster public confidence in the plan.

verb

buttress

support a claim or structure, often with evidence

New archival records buttress the historian's interpretation.

verb

facilitate

make a process easier

Shared terminology can facilitate cooperation across research teams.

adjective

pertinent

directly relevant and important to the matter

The committee requested only the evidence pertinent to the safety review.

adjective

efficacious

producing the intended result

The treatment was efficacious in reducing symptoms during the trial.

adjective

benign

gentle or medically not harmful

Tests showed that the growth was benign, not cancerous.

adjective

innocuous

seemingly or actually harmless; unlikely to offend

The isolated error looked innocuous, but repeated errors changed the result.

verb

impede

slow or obstruct progress

Heavy sediment can impede the flow of water without stopping it completely.

verb

thwart

successfully prevent a plan or attempt

Early detection thwarted the attempted cyberattack.

verb

undermine

gradually weaken confidence, authority, or effectiveness

Unreported conflicts of interest can undermine trust in a study.

verb

exacerbate

make an existing problem more severe

A prolonged drought could exacerbate the region's water shortage.

adjective

adverse

unfavorable or working against success

The launch was delayed by adverse weather conditions.

adjective

detrimental

causing a harmful effect

Chronic sleep loss is detrimental to attention and memory.

adjective

insidious

gradually harmful in a way that is difficult to notice

The invasive insect had an insidious effect that became visible only after several seasons.

Precision locks

Close words share a direction. The correct word matches the exact relationship.

beneficial / constructive / conduciveBeneficial produces a good result. Constructive improves or builds. Conducive creates favorable conditions for a result.
pragmatic / efficaciousPragmatic describes a workable approach. Efficacious describes something proven capable of producing its intended result.
bolster / buttressBoth strengthen. Buttress often points to evidence supporting a claim; bolster is broader and can strengthen confidence, reputation, or a position.
facilitate / impede / thwartFacilitate makes easier. Impede slows or obstructs. Thwart successfully stops an attempt.
benign / innocuousBenign often describes a gentle condition or a noncancerous growth. Innocuous means harmless or unlikely to cause offense and can imply something merely looks harmless.
adverse / detrimental / insidiousAdverse means unfavorable. Detrimental directly causes harm. Insidious causes hidden or gradual harm.
impede / undermine / exacerbateImpede slows progress. Undermine weakens a foundation, claim, or confidence. Exacerbate makes an existing problem worse.
