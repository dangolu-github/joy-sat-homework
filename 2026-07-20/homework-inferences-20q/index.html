Inferences — Independent Practice

Joy · Class 14 Homework 1 · 20 questions

Use the Class 13 method without annotations:
  • compress the evidence into a short chain;
  • name the blank's logical job;
  • lock the group, condition, time, variable, direction, and certainty;
  • reject the closest trap by naming the extra evidence it would require.

Question 1

Moderate

Botanists studying a shrub common in fire-prone grasslands collected seeds from several populations. The researchers stored all seeds under the same conditions and provided equal moisture during germination tests. Untreated seeds rarely germinated, and brief exposure to heat alone produced little change. Seeds exposed to chemicals found in smoke, however, germinated at much higher rates even when they had not been heated. In natural grasslands, smoke can spread beyond the patches that burn most intensely. The findings suggest that ______

Which choice most logically completes the text?

  1. A. heat prevents the shrub's seeds from germinating after every grassland fire.
  2. B. smoke-related chemical cues may trigger germination even for some seeds outside the hottest burned patches.
  3. C. all shrubs in fire-prone habitats use identical germination cues.
  4. D. the shrub's seeds can germinate only after being physically damaged.

Question 2

Moderate

Recently catalogued notebooks spanning eight years reveal a less direct process: Veras first proposed a highly centralized building, rejected it after interviews with residents, and tested several courtyard arrangements before choosing the final plan. Dated sketches show that some rejected layouts were reconsidered more than once. The essays accurately describe the evidence behind the finished design but omit these abandoned stages. The notebooks therefore indicate that ______

Which choice most logically completes the text?

  1. A. Veras did not speak with residents until after the building was completed.
  2. B. the centralized design was more popular with residents than the courtyard plan was.
  3. C. the published essays contain no reliable information about the finished design.
  4. D. Veras's public account makes the design process appear more linear than it actually was.

  3. C. bats choose cooler caves because those caves contain more food.
  4. D. every bat in a warm cave leaves at exactly the same time.
