Class summary

What we completed, what went well, and the reasoning habits to strengthen next.

July 20
Covered today We completed the remaining Inferences lesson and guided practice. The main routine was to compress the passage into an Evidence Chain, identify the Blank Job, and use Scope and Strength Lock to choose only the conclusion the text earns. Words in Context was not taught in this class; the 10-question set is a preview for Class 15.
What went well Joy listened attentively, stayed engaged, accepted corrections, and was willing to attempt answers and short summaries. These are useful foundations for making the reasoning process more independent.
Main challenge When a passage contained a discrepancy or several linked ideas, Joy sometimes focused on a surface detail instead of stating the central logical relationship. Some answers also addressed a nearby idea rather than the exact subject of the question.
Reasoning target Before looking at the choices, say the logical chain aloud: What does the passage establish? What exactly must the blank or question answer? When rejecting an option, identify the precise mismatch—wrong subject, missing condition, unsupported certainty, or evidence the passage never gives.
Next steps Use one short point-summary for each key idea, connect those points into a chain, and explain each choice in a complete sentence. Add unfamiliar high-frequency words from the passages to a vocabulary list and use the Vocabulary Hub for optional review.
Homework Homework 1: 20 difficult official Inferences questions. Homework 2: 10-question Words in Context preview for Class 15. Both are assigned for independent work and have not yet been checked.
