Inferences — Annotated Preview

Joy · Class 13 · 10 SAT-style questions

Goal: choose the smallest conclusion that the text actually earns. The annotations show where to look; they do not supply the answer.
Reading-map key:
Skeleton = condensed premises Anchor = transition or punctuation job Scope lock = condition, group, degree, or time limit Trick watch = the most tempting unsupported leap

Question 1

Moderate

Cultural historian Leena Shah studies a collection of commercial seed packets saved by shopkeeper Arun Mehta between 1912 and 1941. Because the packets were designed to be discarded after use, several archives initially dismissed them as advertising scraps. Decades later, however, a regional agricultural museum acquired the collection after researchers showed that changes in the packets’ crop names, illustrations, and planting instructions could help trace shifts in local gardening and food production. Hence, Mehta’s collection illustrates ______

Skeleton: once dismissed → later acquired and used Anchor: Hence asks for a synthesis Scope lock: scholarly value changed over time Trick watch: do not invent preservation difficulty

Which choice most logically completes the text?

  1. A. that contemporary historians cannot study regional food production without access to Mehta’s packets.
  2. B. the difficulty museums generally face when preserving paper objects made for commercial use.
  3. C. that researchers continued to consider disposable packaging useless even after the collection entered the museum.
  4. D. both the low scholarly value once assigned to disposable materials and the later recognition of their possible usefulness.

Question 2

Moderate

Entomologist Pia Moreno exposed groups of leaf-beetle larvae to the odors of different host plants. After the beetles matured, adults tended to approach the plant odor they had encountered as larvae. Moreno also notes that female beetles usually lay eggs on the plants they approach for feeding and that newly hatched larvae feed on the plant where their eggs were laid. Since early odor preference can therefore affect where the next generation begins feeding, the results suggest that ______

Skeleton: larval odor → adult approach → egg site → offspring food Anchor: Since links the chain to the conclusion Scope lock: tended to, not always Trick watch: keep the inference on plant choice, not growth rate

Which choice most logically completes the text?

  1. A. beetles’ preferences for host-plant odors probably disappear once the beetles begin laying eggs.
  2. B. odor exposure during the larval stage may encourage adult beetles to place their offspring on the same type of host plant.
  3. C. larvae exposed to a preferred plant odor grow more quickly than larvae exposed to an unfamiliar odor.
  4. D. adult beetles prefer every locally available plant to all plants found outside their original habitat.

Question 3

Moderate

Some archaeologists have treated the painted ceramic vessels found in elite burial sites as evidence of the dishes people commonly used in nearby settlements. However, household excavations in those settlements have yielded large numbers of plain cooking and storage pots but very few painted vessels. Chemical analysis also indicates that the bright pigments on many burial vessels were made from minerals imported at considerable cost. These findings cast doubt on the idea that ______

Skeleton: tombs = many painted vessels; homes = mostly plain pots Anchor: However overturns the first interpretation Theme: representativeness of surviving objects Trick watch: “not representative” does not prove a specific symbolic meaning

Which choice most logically completes the text?

  1. A. elite households in the settlements owned no plain ceramic vessels.
  2. B. imported pigments were less expensive for pottery workshops than local minerals were.
  3. C. ceramic objects placed in elite burials provide a realistic picture of ordinary household vessel use.
  4. D. artisans selected painted designs mainly to record specific political events.

Question 4

Difficult

To study lichen growth on exposed stone, ecologists placed half of a set of identical stone tiles beneath translucent covers and left the other half uncovered. The covers both reduced the amount of sunlight reaching the tiles and slowed evaporation, so the covered surfaces remained wetter. After six months, lichens occupied a greater percentage of the covered tiles than of the uncovered tiles. Because the two environmental conditions changed together, the result most strongly suggests that ______

Skeleton: less light + more moisture → more lichen Anchor: Because states the reasoning limit Scope lock: two variables changed together Trick watch: do not assign the effect to only one variable

Which choice most logically completes the text?

  1. A. the study cannot determine whether reduced light, increased moisture, or their combination produced the additional growth.
  2. B. all lichen species grow fastest in low light regardless of how much moisture is available.
  3. C. the covers must have released a chemical that directly accelerated lichen growth.
  4. D. sunlight never affects lichen growing on naturally exposed stone.

Question 5

Difficult

Geoarchaeologists extracted sediment cores from an ancient harbor to investigate when copper working began there. Because slag is produced when ore is heated rather than through the ordinary transport of finished metal objects, the researchers treated slag as evidence of local production. Layers deposited more than 2,700 years ago contained no copper-smelting slag. By contrast, layers deposited between 2,650 and 2,600 years ago contained slag whose isotopic profile matched copper ore from nearby deposits. The researchers did not sample inland settlements outside the harbor district. The evidence therefore supports the conclusion that ______

Skeleton: older layer = no slag; later layer = local-ore slag Anchor: By contrast marks the first positive evidence Scope lock: harbor district; no later than 2,600 years ago Trick watch: one sampled location cannot prove a region-wide pattern

Which choice most logically completes the text?

  1. A. copper working was widespread throughout the entire region more than 2,700 years ago.
  2. B. at least some copper-working activity occurred in the harbor district by about 2,600 years ago.
  3. C. copper working had replaced every other craft practiced in the harbor district by 2,650 years ago.
  4. D. the harbor’s metalworkers depended primarily on ore imported from distant regions.

Question 6

Difficult

A fourteenth-century port charter is explicitly addressed to the city’s residents and is written chiefly in their local language. Yet the document also includes a Latin translation, promises stable tariff rates to merchants arriving from three neighboring states, and bears a seal that those states’ courts recognized in commercial disputes. Such seals commonly authenticated documents circulated beyond their place of issue. Even though the residents are the charter’s named audience, these features make it reasonable to conclude that ______

Skeleton: named residents + foreign-facing language, promises, and seal Anchor: Even though adds a qualified contrast Theme: intended audience, not reader reaction Trick watch: foreign-facing details do not prove foreign opposition

Which choice most logically completes the text?

  1. A. the charter was copied directly from a document issued by one of the neighboring states.
  2. B. most city residents were unable to understand the charter’s local-language version.
  3. C. foreign merchants objected to every tariff policy announced in the charter.
  4. D. the charter was designed to communicate with audiences beyond the city’s residents as well.

Question 7

Difficult

Paleontologists mapped fossil mollusk shells across a former coastal plain to reconstruct the location of an ancient shoreline. Most shells now occur several kilometers inland, a pattern that might appear to mark the mollusks’ original habitat. The shells are durable enough to survive transport while retaining features that allow the species to be identified. However, geological evidence shows that later river channels cut through coastal deposits, transported large quantities of sediment, and redeposited shells farther inland. This evidence indicates that ______

Skeleton: shells inland now; rivers later moved coastal material inland Anchor: However weakens the simple location reading Scope lock: current distribution may not equal original habitat Trick watch: uncertainty is not proof that the first explanation is impossible

Which choice most logically completes the text?

  1. A. the shells’ present distribution may not accurately identify where the mollusks originally lived.
  2. B. the ancient shoreline was necessarily located several kilometers inland from the modern coast.
  3. C. mollusks were entirely absent from the ancient coastal deposits before the rivers formed.
  4. D. river activity on the coastal plain ended before any mollusk shells were deposited.

Question 8

Difficult

Researchers detected the same unusual mixture of minerals in dust on a mountain glacier and in soil from the valley below. Because this mixture is rare in other regional rocks, its independent occurrence at both sites is unlikely. One explanation is that seasonal winds lift valley soil onto the glacier. Another is that meltwater carries mineral particles from the glacier into the valley, where the particles become incorporated into soil. Although the proposed directions of movement differ, either explanation suggests that ______

Skeleton: valley → glacier OR glacier → valley Anchor: either explanation asks for the shared conclusion Scope lock: some material; same unusual mixture Trick watch: do not choose one pathway as proven

Which choice most logically completes the text?

  1. A. windborne mineral dust is more harmful to glacier ice than meltwater-carried particles are.
  2. B. meltwater is always the primary means by which minerals move through mountain environments.
  3. C. at least some mineral material in the glacier dust and valley soil may come from a common source.
  4. D. every mineral present on the glacier must occur in identical proportions throughout the valley.

Question 9

Difficult

Coastal engineers sometimes install oyster-reef structures offshore so that the structures reduce wave energy before waves reach a marsh edge, thereby limiting erosion. In narrow tidal channels, however, structures made from comparable materials and built to similar heights can constrict water flow and increase current speed, which may accelerate erosion along the channel banks. Because the structures themselves are broadly similar across the two settings, the two observations suggest that oyster-reef structures ______

Skeleton: open shore → less erosion; narrow channel → more erosion Anchor: however creates opposite effects Scope lock: effect depends on placement Trick watch: the text compares locations, not reef size or growth rate

Which choice most logically completes the text?

  1. A. benefit marshes only when the structures are large and harm marshes only when they are small.
  2. B. cause oysters to grow faster in tidal channels than they do along open shorelines.
  3. C. should never be installed near any location where water moves through a channel.
  4. D. may reduce or increase erosion depending on where the structures are placed.

Question 10

Difficult

A medieval chronicler rarely describes rulers’ private grief, and modern readers may therefore assume that the chronicler viewed such grief as unimportant. Yet the same chronicler records public funerals in detail, showing that death itself was not excluded from the work. Literary historian Nadiya Okafor cautions that chronicles of the period were conventionally organized around public deeds and moral lessons rather than private emotion. Moreover, personal letters written by several rulers discussed in the chronicle contain explicit expressions of mourning. Scholars who ignore these genre conventions therefore ______

Skeleton: chronicle omits grief; genre omits private emotion; letters show grief Anchor: therefore asks for the risk created by ignoring context Theme: historical genre expectations versus modern expectations Trick watch: no evidence of a secret political motive

Which choice most logically completes the text?

  1. A. are likely to confuse the actions of one medieval ruler with those of another.
  2. B. should disregard personal letters when evaluating the emotional lives of medieval rulers.
  3. C. risk misreading the chronicler’s silence by applying expectations that may be inappropriate to the genre and period.
  4. D. can prove that the chronicler concealed criticism of the rulers’ political decisions.
