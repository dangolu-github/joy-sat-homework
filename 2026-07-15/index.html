Class summary & reflection

The main evidence-matching strategy from class and the next step for practice.

July 15
Covered today We learned how to match a claim with the exact evidence it needs by using Claim Lock, the Direct-Fit Test, and Data Lock. We applied the method to six in-class questions.
What felt difficult The hardest part was keeping the exact evidence relationship clear when a passage or data description contained long, dense information.
Strategy practised Name the claim, predict the evidence it requires, and check every condition: direction, comparison, time, degree, and viewpoint. Then reject any option that is merely related but does not directly prove the claim.
Reflection A choice can be true and still be wrong. The correct answer must complete the exact evidence job in the question.
Next step For each practice question, state the required evidence in one short sentence and explain why the closest distractor fails one condition.
