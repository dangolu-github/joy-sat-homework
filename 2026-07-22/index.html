Class review

What we covered, the strengths observed, and the reading habits to strengthen next.

July 22
Covered today We focused on Words in Context: compressing the sentence or passage, locating the words that control its logic, predicting the blank’s job, and testing each option for meaning, tone, degree, and sentence role. We also practised keeping an unfamiliar word neutral and checking whether an ordinary word has a less familiar meaning.
What went well Joy stayed engaged, listened carefully to direct guidance, acknowledged mistakes, and responded positively to correction. She was willing to look again at the passage and try to identify controlling words.
Main challenge Joy did not yet identify the shortest decisive keywords consistently. This made it harder to connect the passage’s exact evidence to the best option. Several analytical words and close meanings—including words such as exhaustive, dismissive, and orthodox—also needed clarification.
Improvement plan Before opening the choices, Joy should write a short contract for the blank: meaning + tone + degree + role. She should then justify the chosen option and reject the closest trap by naming the exact word or limit it breaks. A small vocabulary set should be reviewed in complete sentence contexts rather than as isolated definitions.
Next step Use the same active-reading routine independently: mark the controlling noun or verb, the logic anchor, and any limit word; then compare every answer with the contract. This will support both Words in Context and precise inference work.
Homework Homework 1: complete 30 difficult Words in Context questions, writing the blank contract before choosing an answer. Homework 2: complete the separate 10-question annotated Text Structure and Purpose preview for Class 16, using the reading maps to track sentence function, overall structure, and author purpose.
